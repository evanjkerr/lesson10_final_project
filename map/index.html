FORECLOSURES

Over the last 70 years in the United States, most legacy cities have been experiencing significant decreases in population. There are many reasons as to why this is the case, and many theories as to the permanent damages this surely caused. But with these population decreases (as well as economic output decreases) those who were left inherited dire circumstances. With the ensuing destruction of many of the housing within these neighborhoods to widen roads and to build the National highway system, a destructive cycle of povery, property depreciation, joblessness, obesity, crime, and drugs became entrenched. Foreclosures, evictions, and homelessness are all symptoms of a larger historic urban disinvestment trend (and federal suburban marketing scheme) that has been happening in the United States since the end of the Great Depression.
